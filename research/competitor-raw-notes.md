# Competitor Research Raw Notes

## Market Size (MMCG Invest, March 2026)
- US glamping market: ~$738M in 2024, on track for $1.5B by 2030 (12-13% CAGR)
- US = 83% of North American glamping revenue, 21% of global market
- Global glamping market: $3.4-3.8B in 2024, projected $6-7.5B by 2030
- Glamping = only 2.5% of the $28B US camping market but growing 2x as fast
- Glampers spend $251/day vs $200 camping average
- Cabins/pods: 43-48% of market revenue; luxury tents: 25-30%; treehouses: fastest-growing at 11.4% CAGR
- 18-32 age cohort: 43.9% of glamping revenue
- Direct bookings: 54.7% of channel mix
- 745 glamping properties, 3,409 units tracked across all 50 states (mid-2025)
- Industry ADR: $251/night in 2025 (up 21% from $207 in 2023)

## Key Players (Branded Operators)

### Under Canvas
- 13 safari-style camps + new Outdoor Collective brand
- Majority-owned by KSL Capital Partners (Denver PE)
- $100M+ acquisition in Dec 2018 + $25M expansion in 2022 + $50M in 2025
- Revenue: $115M+ (estimated)
- New locations 2025: Columbia River Gorge, Yosemite; White Mountains NH slated June 2026
- Positioning: Premium/luxury, near iconic national parks, safari tent aesthetic

### AutoCamp
- 8-9 locations, 35-108 units per site (custom Airstreams, luxury tents, cabins)
- $115M commitment from Whitman Peterson
- Exclusive partnership with Hilton Honors loyalty program (Feb 2024)
- 100-property long-term target
- Positioning: Airstream/design-forward, Hilton partnership = mainstream luxury

### Postcard Cabins (formerly Getaway House)
- 29 outposts, 1,200+ tiny cabins
- Acquired by Marriott International in December 2024 (Goldman Sachs advising)
- Hyatt alliance with Under Canvas through Mr. & Mrs. Smith
- Positioning: Accessible cabin escapes, digital detox, proximity to cities

### Collective Retreats
- Revenue: ~$13.9M estimated
- Positioning: Luxury pop-up glamping on private/public land, experiential
- B2B model: partners with landowners/venues

## Key Players (Marketplace Platforms)

### Hipcamp
- 500,000+ listings
- 8 million users
- $98.6M total funding (Series C led by Index Ventures and BOND Capital)
- Valuation: $300M+
- Revenue: ~$30.9M estimated
- Commission: 10% from hosts
- Positioning: "Land sharing" - private land, farms, ranches, vineyards
- App-first, camping-to-glamping spectrum
- International expansion: AU, UK

### Glamping Hub
- Founded 2013, Seville Spain HQ
- Revenue: $6.7M (2024), up from $5M (2023)
- Funding: $3.1M total
- 37 employees
- 18,000 listings across 115 countries
- 64% of traffic from US
- Positioning: Curated global unique outdoor accommodations
- Acquired (exit noted on CBInsights)

### Tentrr
- Filed Chapter 7 BANKRUPTCY in 2023
- Overexpanded during COVID
- Was charging ~35% commission (hosts kept 65%)
- Avg nightly rate ~$150
- Revenue: ~$5.5M at peak
- Cautionary tale: fragility of platform models with unsophisticated host operators

### The Dyrt
- 30 million campers used platform in 2024
- #1 camping app in US
- Freemium model: free basic, Pro subscription ($35.99/year)
- Focus: campsite reviews, discovery (not primarily booking)
- Positioning: Camping information/community, not glamping-specific

## Hotel Chain Moves
- Marriott acquired Postcard Cabins (Getaway House) Dec 2024
- Hilton partnered with AutoCamp (Hilton Honors integration)
- Hyatt allied with Under Canvas through Mr. & Mrs. Smith
- All three major US hotel loyalty programs now have glamping inventory

## Airbnb
- "Unique Stays" category: treehouses, yurts, houseboats, castles, caves
- Estimated 100,000+ unique/unusual listings in US
- Commission: 3% host + 14-16% guest (total ~17-19%)
- Positioning: Everything, everywhere - unique stays is a filter, not a focus

## VRBO (Expedia Group)
- Subscription model for hosts: $499/year OR 8% commission per booking
- Focus: whole-home vacation rentals, family travel
- Less unique/experiential, more traditional vacation rental
- Positioning: Family vacation rentals, beach/ski/lake houses

## Key Trends
- Hotel chains entering glamping = legitimization but also commoditization risk
- Direct booking share (54.7%) shows consumer preference to bypass OTAs
- Treehouses fastest-growing segment (11.4% CAGR, highest per-night premiums)
- 18-32 cohort dominates revenue - Instagram-native, experience-over-stuff
- Cabins/pods most stable revenue (year-round operability)
